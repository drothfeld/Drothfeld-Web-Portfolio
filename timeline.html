2012 - 2016
I graduated from UC Santa Cruz with a BA in Computer Science a quarter early in March 2016. For my undergraduate capstone project, I built a web application called GTC (Guitar Tab Creator) using the Web2py framework. I implemented an interactive digital guitar that played the note a user presses on the neck, and then records the note into a TAB. The user is given sheet music editing tools that can be used with the interactive guitar to create songs. Songs can be saved/downloaded and commented on (GTC can also play the song back in audio form).

An unexpected turn of events brought me back to school to get my Masters's Degree. I am part of the MS Computational Media (CM) program at Baskin Engineering at UC Santa Cruz. CM is a relatively new department that is focused on how computer science, humanities, and the arts, work together to create new areas of research and artifacts. My current research is about measuring the effectiveness of using a touch-interface specifically designed to create an intuitive experience for senior citizens taking the Trail Making Test (A neurological assessment that measures cognitive ability). While conducting research and taking classes, I'm employed by the university as a Graduate Teaching Assistant for undergraduate Computer Science courses.
